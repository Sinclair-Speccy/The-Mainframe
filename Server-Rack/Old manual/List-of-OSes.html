QBASIC OS Guide

Emulation Software

MS-DOS Emulation

DOSBox: A must-have for DOS enthusiasts, DOSBox allows you to effortlessly run classic DOS games and applications. Its versatility and compatibility make it an ideal choice for recreating the MS-DOS experience.

86box: Step further into PC nostalgia with 86box, which emulates not only DOS but also early versions of Windows and vintage PC hardware.

Mac OS Emulation

SheepShaver: If you're an Apple aficionado, SheepShaver's your golden ticket. It's perfect for emulating PowerPC Mac OS environments and allows you to enjoy the charms of Mac OS 9 and earlier on your modern hardware.

Basilisk II: Delve into the world of vintage Mac OS, including System 7 and early Mac OS 8 versions, with Basilisk II. It's an excellent choice if you're keen on exploring Apple's retro software catalogue.

PCE/macplus: Journey back to the early days of the Macintosh with PCE/macplus. Immerse yourself in the Macintosh 128K environment and explore the simplicity of its early graphical interface.

PearPC: For a unique Macintosh emulation experience, PearPC emulates PowerPC-based Macs, allowing you to explore older versions of Mac OS X.

Multi-System Emulation

QEMU: This Swiss Army knife of emulators supports a wide array of operating systems, including various versions of Windows, Linux, BSD, and even some exotic ones. It's like having an emulator playground at your fingertips.

VirtualBox: While often used for modern virtualization, VirtualBox can also house legacy operating systems. It's versatile and works well for running a variety of vintage OSes.

Commodore Emulation

VICE: For the Commodore fans among us, VICE is a blessing. This emulator covers the Commodore 64, VIC-20, and other classic models, ensuring that you can enjoy your favorite Commodore software and games.

WinUAE: If the Amiga holds a special place in your heart, WinUAE is the emulator you're seeking. It accurately emulates the Commodore Amiga series, enabling you to rediscover its software library and games.

Miscellaneous Emulation

LisaEm: Delve into the rarefied world of the Apple Lisa with LisaEm. Experience the historical Apple Lisa interface and appreciate its significance in the evolution of personal computing.
