Woah, definitions...

Simply put, a design philosophy is a set of guiding principles that influence and lead the approach towards designing products, systems, or services. It reflects the designer’s values, beliefs, and strategies, and it shapes the aesthetics, functionality, and usability of the design. Some examples of this can include the Snow White Design Language and Hartmut Esslinger’s Frog Design.

However in this case, we apply the concept of "design philosophy" to character design which can change the definiton a bit to the following:

A guiding principle that influences how a designer approaches their work. Real life design philosophies can be applied to characters as well.

Although I don't usually like calling the way I design my characters a "design philosophy". I like to see it as a way for me to design characters so it makes sense to me. The concept of design philosophy was merely used as an example
