Introduction

Before you read this, I want to express that this is not really for your average person who simply appreciates the designs and doesn’t care so much. It’s written and put together from issues I’ve seen and I guess experienced while also trying to originally research about the Netrunner and Deja Vu incident that happened with Futaba and Netrunner and Deja Vu before coming across this section on a site that talks about the incident, so I'm trying to make sense of that section along with some others.

Origins and Development of OS-tans

What people may know as "canon" are the Futaba and Madobe universes but there is no real canon. What you are actually seeing are the more "accepted" designs from Futaba Channel's "2D back" imageboard. OS-tans are characters that everyone has created and "raised" by Toshiaki (so-called nameless, anons like on 4chan) on Futaba Channel.

Historical Context and Popularization

OS Musume, also known as OS-tan (both terms are interchangeable), began in the summer of 2003. The concept started with the personification of "Windows Me" as a girl specifically in a maid outfit. This character became popular, and many users drew pictures of her on the 2D back imageboard. Following Me-tan's success, other OS versions like Windows 2000, XP, 95, 98, and 98SE were also personified.

The Concept of Canon and Interpretations

The term "canon" in the context of OS-tan characters is often associated with the designs and stories from the Futaba universe and to an extent, the Madobe universe (they weren’t made at Futaba). However, it is important to understand that there is no official canon. For example the design of Windows 2000 can be said to have multiple designers that contributed to her similar to 98-tan and other characters whose designers are unknown.

Artistic Contributions and Variations

Various artists contributed their own designs and interpretations, leading to multiple versions and variations of each character. Windows 95-tan has multiple designs, including a modest woman in a pink kimono by someone supposedly named Shiru Aki and other interpretations that emerged over time.

Community Influence and Interpretations

Futaba Channel played a significant role in popularizing OS-tan characters. Many designs and concepts originated or were heavily refined there, giving them a certain level of perceived authority over the characters. However, the collaborative and open nature of the creation process means that there is not an official canon that can be attributed to any one source. The characters were most likely never meant to get really popular outside of Futaba due to the threads on Futaba Channel disappearing once they were too old or the fact Futaba Channel is in Japanese, not really meant for outsiders. To me it looks like characters were left up to interpretation by their creators intentionally, leaving others to piece together ideas. Despite this they gained significant attention, even reaching Western audiences with projects like a certain wiki which aimed to expand upon what Futaba Channel made (though it did not go well).

Uncertainty and "Headcanons"

We will most likely never see the original thread where ME-tan was shown for the first time, therefore we will never really know what was intended of her and whenever the info about her on other wikis is one hundred correct besides details that seem consistent across artworks of her and others.

Someone brought up to me the idea of "headcanons" and I feel that it applies to the Futaba Channel too. Since wikis are not always 100 correct and the Japanese Futaba fan wiki stated that itself might have a lack of accuracy it suggests that many details could be considered "headcanons" — personal interpretations rather than accurate representations.

Community Contributions and Diversity

Fan communities and wikis often compile and organize information about OS-tan characters. These sources sometimes present certain designs or story elements as "canon" based on their popularity or acceptance “within Futaba” as different wikis or fan sites may have differing views on what constitutes the "true" or official version of a character.

Conclusion

Artists, writers, and creators at Futaba but also outside of Futaba (eventually) collaboratively developed the designs and expanded the OS-tan universe through drawings, comics, games, and other media. This collaborative creation process made OS-tan a shared property among the community members both in and out of Futaba Channel.

It can be said that there are as many OS-tan worlds as there are artists. If you go to the archived version of Cool.ne.jp and scroll down you will find this:

自作絵祭りの流れに乗って、もはや二次裏のスタンダードとなったOS娘ワールド。そのバリエーションは十人十様、まさに絵師の数だけOSたんワールドが存在しているともいえる。 そして、その集大成とも言えるFLASHが完成！おなじみの「あの歌」にのせて、としあき達のOSたんが動く！さらにはクリックによる隠し要素も満載、としあきならずともFLASHファンなら必見のムービーです。

Which translates roughly to this:

Riding on the trend of self-made picture festivals, OS Girl World has now become the standard for secondary art. There are dozens of variations, and it can be said that there are as many OS-tan worlds as there are artists. And FLASH, which can be said to be the culmination of all of this, is completed! Toshiaki and his friends' OS-tan moves to the tune of the familiar "that song"! Furthermore, there are many hidden elements that can be clicked, making this movie a must-see for any FLASH fan.

OS-tan characters are a result of collective creativity with no single canon. The closest you will get to “canon” are the minor details consistent across artworks and other media with them.

Different artists and communities have contributed to their development, leading to multiple interpretations and variations. While Futaba Channel has played a significant role in shaping and spreading these characters, it does not hold absolute authority over what is considered canon. Fan communities and wikis often reflect this diversity, which can sometimes lead to conflicting or overlapping interpretations of the characters. The "canon" of OS-tans is fluid and can vary depending on the community and the creators involved. It is a shared universe where the collective contributions of the fans define the characters and their narratives rather than a single unchanging storyline so places shouldn't present these characters as "canon" but rather "the more accepted" designs.

Introduction

Some of what is said here will most likely overlap with my first issue...

As you, the reader, may know, there are currently two major wikis about OS-tans, those being the older English wiki and the newer English wiki respectively.

Historical Context and Development

You see, back in the day when the OS-tan boom was happening on Futaba, the older English wiki which is pretty mich the highest on the search result for OS-tans tried to gather information on them the best they could by relying mainly on art of the characters. At the time, this would have been doable, but as OSC's wiki aged, they started to mash this info about the Futaba characters into their own canon called “Annex” which was meant to build upon what Futaba had. This led to a mess because with everything being muddled up as one big mess of “canon” and the Annex project in one, no one is really going to know if what is written is true or not. Turn the clock to the 2020s and you’ll see that some pages link to another newer English wiki.

Recent Discussions

Flash forward to May 2024 and then there was a discussion in the r/OStan discord where it was said that Me-tan’s name really is not Emui but rather “Meko” along with a discussion with newly found information for XP-tan.

The whole “Meko” name claims boil down to the fact that on Me-tan’s creator’s site there is art they did with some of them having “Meko” in the file name. Some of them also have “Metan”. This led to me wondering if the entry for her at the “@wiki”. This other English wiki uses another wiki for their info on OS-tan which is called the “@wiki” but the whole “@wiki” thing isn’t the wiki’s actual name, that's actually the software that is being used for the wiki similar to how you have MediaWiki for Wikipedia. The wiki itself is called "ふたば★二次裏@wiki" aka Futaba★Secondary Backstage@wiki. The "@wiki" part is what I assume an ending of sorts the software puts on the end of all the wikis as you can see more of these at the actual site for the @wiki (https://atwiki.jp/) software but also the about page for the wiki itself:

そのためJavaScriptの使える@wikiを選びましたが使うためには管理権限が必要であり、他の人が作っているところでそれを要求するわけにもいかなかったので結局独自に作ることにしました。
I wanted to use JavaScript to template the pages so that I could keep them in the same format as possible. Is. For that reason, I chose @wiki, which supports JavaScript, but in order to use it, I needed administrative privileges, and I couldn't ask for that when someone else was making it, so I ended up creating my own.

Investigating the Sources

Anyway, back to Me-tan’s real name, I looked around her wiki page and translated stuff which showed that it had “Emui”, but no sources as to where this name came from. I'm starting to think that it might be a fan name people gave her at Futaba that latched on because the English long E sound is usually I in other languages, the E in "M.E" is pronounced as so, M is pronounced "Em" so in Japanese people turned it into "Emu" and then long E turns into "i".

If Meko wasn't her actual name nor the name the creator endorsed... Why would her creator name her files "Meko" and why is it not mentioned on her creator’s site? Meko being her "name", even if it’s literally just Me-child, is most likely correct because no one knows where the whole Emui thing came from. The English wikis and I guess the Japanese wiki too ( don't really think so) pose this information as absolute information when it has never been verified when this is her name or not.

Evaluating Source Accuracy

I looked at both the Futaba★Secondary Backstage and Seesaa wiki without doing any alterations to see if I could back my "100 not correct" info claim up and saw that both could logically be edited without an account. Now, the newer English wiki has a section where pages have content that is "challenged" meaning that it may not be correct and users can help clear them by providing appropriate references that either confirm or infirm the statements. They also have "stubs" for articles that only have minimal information and have the potential to be significantly expanded.

Since they are using these sources for "accurate" information, anyone could easily bullshit information on them and no one would bat an eye to it because "it's from Futaba" or "it's from Japan".

One example is Futababa (Windows 2.x-tan). She has an entry on the newer English wiki which is a stub and these sections are challenged:

  1. Futababa is depicted as the middle sister of Oichi (1.0) and Ōbaba (3.1) and also a friend.
  2. Futababa (Japanese: ふた婆) is an OS-tan that depicts Windows 2.1x, or Windows 2.x in general.
  3. She appeared in the Spring is Here! OS Festival (春だ一番！ＯＳ祭り) adult only dōjinshi, which could be bought during the 10th Futaba School Festival held on May 3, 2015, and which may be where she debuted.

Someone who wanted to say this stuff is "accurate" could easily lie by editing either the Seesaa wiki or the Futaba★Secondary Backstage and no one would try to confirm the accuracy most likely, as you could effectively lie that it was from a person from Japan or Futaba themselves who added the "new" info. An example of this would be like me editing the pages to say she is the middle sister of Oichi (1.0) and Ōbaba (3.1) and also a friend, is an OS-tan that depicts Windows 2.1x, or Windows 2.x in general, debuted on May 3 2015, waited a long while then edit the newer English wiki entry to say something along the lines of "her page got recently updated by the people of Futaba and it's correct".

These issues also apply to pages like 3.1-tan's page ("She appeared in a single image (6-c) of the Trouble Windows Opening released in 2004, as the girl inside a CRT screen labelled 3.1, though with a different design, notably with blue eyes"), Inu-T's page ("She appeared in a single image (6-c) of the Trouble Windows Opening released in 2004, as the girl with the blue ponytail hair, though she didn’t have dog ears.") and most likely any of the stub pages, if not all of them really.

Further Complications

Go forward to very early June and a group chat I am in were discussing stuff where I told the users there that 95-tan did some bad stuff to Reobo (Mac OS X Leopard-kun who looks like a child) which I felt should be mentioned in a content warning for one of the external links on his the newer English wiki page as this was found on the Futaba★Secondary Backstage page for Reobo. This information lingered around for a while until 6 days later when I was bored and looked around the Futaba★Secondary Backstage itself. And whoop-de-doo it turns out that the information on the Futaba★Secondary Backstage may not even be 100% correct itself despite coming from Japan itself! Why?

On the about page for the Futaba★Secondary Backstage it says this:

当wikiはふたば★ちゃんねるの二次裏のキャラや知識をまとめておくのを目的とします。しかしながら当wikiに書かれている記事が公式設定というものではなく、書かれていたから有効というものでもありません。
The purpose of this wiki is to summarize the characters and knowledge of the secondary backstage of Futaba★Chan. However, the articles on this wiki are not the official settings, nor are they valid just because they were written.

Let alone the wiki isn't even by Futaba themselves. It was made by one person who worked on a similar wiki that disappeared and wanted to not see their favorite pages disappear again:

これを作る以前は「ふたば★虹板Wiki企画」に二次裏キャラのページをいくつか寄稿していました。そこが急になくなったことで自分の記事が否定されたような気がして、心の中にモヤモヤとしたものを抱え込んでしまったので、最初はそれを晴らしたかっただけでした。
Before making this, I had contributed several pages of secondary characters to the "Futaba★Nijiboard Wiki Project". When it suddenly disappeared, I felt like my article was being rejected, and I had a feeling of confusion in my heart, so at first, I just wanted to clear it up.

This made me realize that this information I found about 95-tan doing stuff to a character that looks like a kid may not even be correct or true, which to me leaves the question "Which wiki is more accurate?" The answer? I don't know anymore. OS-tan Collection’s wiki is already an aged mess of canon and fanon mixed in with one another and I presume that the newer English wiki was unaware that this info may not be correct but took it anyway. Sure, the newer English wiki may have sources unlike OS-tan Collections, but how accurate are those sources?

It feels like to me that they were never meant to get really popular, and they were left up to interpretation by their creators intentionally by having vague info. A lot of my thoughts about info not being one hundred percent correct boils down to the fact that we will most likely never see the threads and have to rely on what already exists like the Futaba★Secondary Backstage itself, despite the fact it says info on there may not be accurate.
