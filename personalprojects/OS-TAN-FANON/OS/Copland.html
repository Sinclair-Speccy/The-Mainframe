Character

Copland Nu is a man born from an unknown digital realm with wavy, shoulder-length very light lilac hair and lilac eyes. Copland wears a cape that envelops his entire body which is blue. His cape includes a hood. Underneath the cape, he has a white vest with thin notch lapels and stamped metal buttons, a black belt, black pants, grey boots and lilac gloves. He started off as optimistic and ambitious, driven by a vision of progress and innovation, aiming to bring positive change but started to face difficulties. Copland faced adversity with unwavering determination.

He faced issues like instability and fragility as he strived to push the boundaries but it seemed too late, as it wasn't long until he recognised that his initial purpose was not working out, causing him distress and forcing him to reevaluate his path. The higher-ups of The Infinite Loop ended up sending him off as he proved to be too expensive to keep around. He is determined to prove himself and rise above the setbacks, despite being left struggling with feelings of inadequacy, frustration, and a sense of unfinished business, often questioning his own abilities.

He's now part of the Retrograde Conglomerate after being a wanderer for so long and Taligent convinced him to join them. At times when he doesn't want to be known who he is he uses the name "Maxwell".

Family and Relationships

System 7 and 7.5

Out of all The Infinite Loop members, Copland gets along well with Pleiades and Amadea and were the ones to know him well enough before he was kicked out.

Trivia

It is said that Copland comes from the future, 2030 to be exact but no one actually knows if this is true or not.

Abilities

Copland-kun has a knack for handling multiple tasks simultaneously.

References and sources

  • Wikipedia entry on Copland.
  • He also represents Copland's tentatively planned successor, codenamed Gershwin.
  • Ellen Hancock was the reason why the Copland project was killed of. After only a few months, she realised that the situation was hopeless, and given current development and engineering it would never ship at all.
  • When Nagel left Apple , Apple insisted that the operating system would ship eventually despite the loss of a key player. However, Nagel’s departure came soon after Apple posted a staggering $740 million loss.
  • The last name "Nu" comes from the Nukernel, a microkernel that was developed at Apple Computer during the early 1990s. It was the basis for the Copland operating system. Copland build D7E1 runs entirely on top of NuKernel, while relying on a Mac OS 7 (this is where him being friends with System 7 and 7.5 comes from) although it is widely unstable, and often crashes upon performing certain tasks.
  • Members of Apple's software QA team joked that, given current resources and the number of bugs in the system, they could clear the program for shipping sometime around 2030.
  • His first name comes from the Copland codename which is named after Aaron Copland, a musician. His other name is Maxwell, an internal code name for Copland.
