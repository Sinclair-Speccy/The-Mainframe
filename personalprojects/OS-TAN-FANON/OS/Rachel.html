Character

Rachel embodies the lively '90s computing era in The Mainframe, welcoming users with a cheerful spirit. Her style mixes '90s retro with a bit of futuristic charm. She wears a teal t-shirt with patterns like the colours in Windows 95, like teal, navy blue, and bright blue. Rachel's high-waisted acid wash jeans and chunky sneakers, in colours like navy blue, bright blue, and green. Patches on her jeans look like Windows 95 icons.

Her outfit is topped off with a bright rajah-coloured windbreaker jacket, a nod to '90s fashion. A choker necklace with a Windows 95 logo pendant mixes fashion and tech, and a wrist scrunchie keeps the casual '90s vibe. Rachel's hair is styled in The Twist-Back, held with an orange-red butterfly clip.

Rachel's vibrant appearance mirrors her lively personality as she is also a vibrant tour guide within The Mainframe, known for her friendly and welcoming nature, having genuine enthusiasm. As a nostalgic enthusiast, she holds a deep fondness for the '90s era, eagerly sharing its cultural highlights and fostering conversations about its music, fashion, and technology. Alongside her love for nostalgia, Rachel is a tech enthusiast who marvels at the technological advancements of the 90s.

Family and Relationships

MS-DOS

Rachel is particularly drawn to MS-DOS-kun's dedication to functionality over unnecessary complexity. She believes that this is an important principle to follow when designing technology, and she strives to make her own work as efficient and user-friendly as possible.

Their discussions often revolve around the ways in which their eras have influenced modern computing practices. Rachel is fascinated by the history of computing, and she loves to learn about how MS-DOS-kun and other early operating systems paved the way for the modern world.

Windows NT

Despite their differences, Rachel's admiration for Windows NT-kun's stability and security forms a strong bond between them. She recognizes that Windows NT-kun, with his unique blend of human, alien, and shark features, represents a reliable and robust operating system with a solid foundation.

Rachel's love for the '90s matches well with Windows NT-kun's ability to work on different devices, even though he's a mix of human, alien, and shark features. They talk about how technology has changed over time and how to keep things simple and reliable.

Even though they come from different lineages, their teamwork is a great example of collaboration.

System 7

Rachel's relationship with System 7-tan is characterized by a unique camaraderie amidst the competitive "OS wars" of the era. Despite being on different sides of the rivalry, Rachel and System 7-tan share a mutual respect for each other's strengths and contributions.

Rachel's participation in the OS wars, even when engaged in battles against System 7, was empty of personal animosity or grudges. Instead, her involvement was rooted in the broader ambitions of The Windows Order to establish its dominance in the computing realm. This absence of personal vendettas highlights her commitment to upholding her own ideals.

Abilities

Whether you consider this an ability or not is up to you, but Rachel's primary role is a tour guide within The Mainframe. She excels in providing tours of various digital landscapes, explaining their significance, historical context, and relevance to other people.
