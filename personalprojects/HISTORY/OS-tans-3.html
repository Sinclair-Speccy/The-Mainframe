1.2 – A Community Effort

Artists draw based on community input, craftsmen create collages and ongoing iterations refine these characters further. Over time this collaborative process has led to the creation of major versions of OS girls while preserving a myriad of minor variations in memory. Ultimately, the growth and evolution of OS-tans is a truly communal effort. By understanding this background it becomes clear that OS-tans meant a lot to the residents of Nijiura.

While mostly unknown to outsiders there have been a few “wikis” from Japan and most likely Nijiura. One of the earliest may be the OS summary site with its first archive going back to September 12th, 2004 meaning it would be 1 year, 1 month, and 6 days after Me-tan was first made. This site not only uses the term “OS Musume” but also explains their origins and provides summaries of the main characters.

The origins listed on this site match up with what I have researched. Characters were drawn by several different artists and gradually became established. In that sense, the “original author” can be considered to be everyone who contributed ideas on the anonymous boards. However, because it is inherently difficult for an individual on such forums to assert copyright, Me-aki and XP-aki stepped forward individually, meaning they legally hold the copyrights to their respective creations.

But if Me and XP had only been published on personal websites, would they have spread across the internet as widely as they did? Probably not. In other words, “OS-tan” was born thanks to the image board known as 2ch and its active users.

  • OS Musume Original Author: Toshiaki
  • Copyright Holders for the Characters: Those who first drew them (Me-aki, XP-aki, etc.)
  • Copyright for Each Derivative Work: The respective creators

Since these events took place on an anonymous forum and involved anthropomorphising Microsoft’s operating systems, the legal aspects are somewhat unclear and complicated.

Artwork was posted, personalities were assigned, further design refinements were made, and many users contributed various elements that gradually shaped the character. Many “Toshiakis” drew OS-tan; some created manga, some wrote stories, and some made Flash animations or games. In this way, the world of OS-tan expanded within the community.

It is important to note that during this period of OS anthropomorphism, 2channel (2ch) was not directly involved. The idea of anthropomorphism had existed long before (think of characters like Magical Tokochan), so arguing over who originally conceived the concept of OS anthropomorphism is futile, the only potential disputes would concern specific design aspects. The site also links to +Nijiura+, another board like Futaba Channel that I will get to later.

The next wiki would be the “Nijiura Character Dictionary.” This wiki is linked on Futaba-info, a site that “supports Futaba without permission” and “mainly conveys various information about Futaba Channel.” While simple, it shows some characters like XP-tan, ME-tan, and mentions “OS Son” for the first time.

The last of these wikis is “Futaba ★ Rainbow Board@Wiki”. It describes itself as a “project support site where we all come together to collect characters and movements from the Futaba Nijiura Board.” The earliest archive for the OS section is October 26, 2006.

Unlike the previous two, this wiki gets into detailed information about each OS-tan, explaining the history behind each character, like 98-tan’s "Hacchan" nickname and Me-tan’s love of leeks and roll cakes. It also explains that Sonata-sensei gave herself the title "Sensei" rather than it being assigned by others.

In 2004, a separate Japanese imageboard called +Nijiura+ embraced the OS-tan phenomenon much like Futaba Channel. However, it’s important not to confuse this site with the Nijiura board on Futaba, as they are two different places.

+Nijiura+ created individual characters for each new release of Mac OS X, inspired by their codenames: Cheetah, Puma, Jaguar, Panther, Tiger, and Leopard. Most of what follows is drawn from older forum posts on OS-tan Collections.
