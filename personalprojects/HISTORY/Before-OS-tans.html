0 - The Concept of Personification Before OS-tans

Before we even get into the origins of OS-tans themselves, we need to go way further back to around the 90s, 1998 to be exact. The name “iMac Girl” may be familiar to some people as this was logically the “first” Moe Anthropomorphism or gijinka drawn by Satoshi "Toy" Igarashi that could fall under the "OS-tan" category. The reason for “first” being in quotations is because a lot of people see iMac Girl as the first ever, but this is actually incorrect.

Depending on how you define technology gijinkas and OS-tans, the first are supposedly for both the PlayStation and Sega Saturn, known as “Game Console Gals” from December 25, 1994, as seen on an earlier archive of his site. If you don't count the Game Console Gals as being the first, then it would be "G3 Girl” from March 24, 1998, who supposedly represents the iMac G3, “Bondi Blue Babe” who dates back to August 1998, and the “iCandies” who date back to March 1999.

During the same year as G3 Girl, OS Idol Win-chan from October 1998 for TECHWIN Magazine was made as some sort of mascot for the magazine. Most people tend to think that she is a catch-all character due to the fact it can be presumed she was never really meant to represent a single OS but rather be a catch-all, as the magazine nor CD-ROM (which can be found on the Internet Archive) never seems to mention if she is a single OS. For me personally, I think her being seen as representing Windows is subjective because I feel she represents the magazine only and is not really related to Windows itself?

Similar to the above in 1999, you had the mascot for the "Linux MoeMoe Project" called Rimo, though that is not her official name. She was created because the creator wished to add the "moe" (cute and endearing) element to the Linux penguin mascot and imagined this character could be treated similarly to the Linux Penguin but in a Japanese manga style.

What does this site have to do with anything else though if it is a site that exists as a platform for exploring and developing "moe-style" applications for Linux? It actually links to its own moe gijinka content and other moe gijinkas by other creators. Let's talk about moe gijinkas on the site itself first.

Language Musume ("Language Girls")

"Language Musume" (ランゲージ娘) is a project from the early 2000s that personifies various computer languages and is part of the "Linux MoeMoe Project." Each character represents a different programming language, but the real aim is to build an open and free shared world where each operating system is compared to a “school” and each language is compared to a “female student” who attends the school.

The project attempted to convince other users to join and make their own characters, even if they did not have any art skill but could contribute written descriptions. It was even encouraged to develop games using these characters, contributing to the creative and shared world. I'm not going to list every single character; however, 18 were made in total, with the project lasting 6 months and 21 days. A promotional movie in the style of an anime opening was even created which I found myself and uploaded on YouTube.

The Language Musume page links to other moe gijinkas not related to their site as well.

Other Moe Gijinkas

This page was created as a tribute to the diverse characters and the researchers behind them, featuring technology and software that have been personified as girls. I suggest you look at the sites yourself listed, but if you don't want to look at the list, the characters include:

  • Magical Toko-chan (BSD)
  • Solaris-chan (Solaris)
  • Unix Maid System Five-chan (Unix System-V)
  • Direct☆Girl Wintel Miku-chan (Windows)
  • Lina-chan with Linux (Linux)
  • DIVE☆GIRL BigBlue Marin-chan (OS/2Warp4)
  • Vine-chan with Magical Rod (VineLinux)
  • Object-Oriented Girl Ruby-chan with Perl Sister (Ruby & Perl)
  • JABAKO Project (JAVA)
  • Project W3C-ko
  • Mario-chan (Sigmarion)
  • eZaurus-chan (eZaurus)

Each of these characters has unique traits and represents the technology they are based on in creative ways. For example, Dive Girl BigBlue Marin-chan stands out for her setting, featuring both human and non-human characters. Some of these characters like Magical Toko go beyond into things like world-building. These projects show the deep enthusiasm and creativity of early fan communities, which laid the ground for later anthropomorphic movements like OS-tans.

That being said, there are actually quite a few gijinkas related to technology and software prior to the creation of OS-tans themselves. Each one stands out in its own way, whether through their art style, backstory, or the universes they are in.
