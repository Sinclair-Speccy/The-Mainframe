Introduction

OS-tans are moe personification representations of computer operating systems, often depicted as anime-style characters. These characters first emerged from Japanese online communities in the early 2000s, originating on the Nijiura board of Futaba Channel, a board with "no rules."

When you think of OS-tans, the first thing that likely comes to mind is the “main cast” such as XP-tan, 95-tan, Me-tan, 2K-tan, and so on. These characters are often considered the “canon” characters within the OS-tan community. For the average fan, these characters might just be admired for their designs or personalities. However, various OS-tan communities have shown flaws and issues in how information is presented, how research is conducted, and how the characters are interpreted. To address these issues which are part of the history itself, it's essential to start from the beginning: how OS-tans even came to be.

Origins and Evolution

OS-tans originated from Japanese online communities in the early 2000s, particularly from Futaba Channel's Nijiura board, known for its chaotic and collaborative nature. The suffix "-tan" is a Japanese honorific that is a childlike or affectionate mispronunciation of "-chan," which is itself a diminutive and endearing suffix often used for close friends, young children, or cute characters. Although widely known as “OS-tan" in the western fandom, the term “XX-tan” has tended to be avoided at Futaba, where the preferred terms are “OS Musume” or "OS Girl" with the male version being “OS Son”. This shift in terminology is said to be due to a change in attitude towards the characters—from caring for them to loving them—but also drama involving Déjà Vu Artworks and Netrunner who either tried to claim ownership of them, or something else.

The concept of moe personification isn’t entirely new, but it was Futaba Channel that popularised the trend. The Nijiura board, while notorious for its NSFW content, is also known for its unspoken rules regarding image use. Members understand that images posted can be freely used as references, modified, or incorporated into stories. This collaborative effort, where images are often edited and transformed into new creations, played a crucial role in the birth and evolution of OS-tans.

For example, Me-tan, the first OS-tan, was created on August 6, 2003, when someone commented on a Windows Me System Properties image, saying, "But Windows Me is cute even though it's no good." This comment led to the personification of Windows Me, marking the beginning of the OS-tan phenomenon. The community quickly embraced this idea, and soon, various versions of other OS-tans like 2K-tan, XP-tan, and 95-tan were created by different artists, or "Toshiaki," as they are sometimes called. These artists and craftsmen, though anonymous, played a significant role in shaping the OS-tan characters through their collaborative and chaotic creative process.

The Nijiura community operates on a system where images are posted, responded to, and further developed through collaboration, with community members contributing by adding stories, attributes, and designs to the characters through their responses. This collaborative process involves artists drawing based on community input, craftsmen creating collages, and further iterations that refine the characters. Over time, these contributions lead to the creation of major versions of OS-tans while preserving minor variations in memory, making the growth of OS-tans a truly collective effort involving many Toshiaki.

Character Design and Creation

Each OS-tan character is designed to reflect the features, strengths, and even the bugs or flaws of the operating system they represent. For example, an OS-tan for an older or less stable OS might be depicted as clumsy or fragile, while a more stable OS-tan could be portrayed as confident and reliable. Some artists also use in-jokes or personal preferences for their characters, such as Vistake and CE-tan.

As for who made them, characters like Me-tan and XP-tan have their respective creators, who are Me-aki and XP Kaki-Aki. However, in the early days, it can be said that everyone at Futaba's Nijiura board contributed to making them, as they gave the artists ideas and suggestions. Therefore, it is not just a specific painter who created these characters, but rather an unspecified group of people who collectively nurtured them. The community of Nijiura was deeply involved in the development of OS-tans, with residents contributing ideas and inspiration to painters and craftsmen.

Spread outside of Futaba & the English-speaking community

OS-tan Collections is no doubt the biggest OS-tan place to exist (if you exclude Nijiura as they have never had a formal official wiki). If you were to go ask an OS-tan fan if they knew what OS-tan Collections is, they might say they know of it. Assuming that OSC’s own wiki is correct OS-tan Collections was founded in early March 2005 by FabianN because of his interest in OS-tans. OS-tan Collections grew slowly from then on, remaining a low-profile forum, small compared to Nijura or Wakachan. FabianN would leave in late 2005, leaving the forum to the care of Pitkin and Fedora. It was stagnant during the summer of 2006, receiving less than a post per day and was considered for closing. In the late summer, the arrival of a flock of new members and the addition of the website of Google and Wikipedia would breathe new life into the forum. The expansion of the gallery during late 2006 would make the forum a gateway for people interested in OS-tans. The addition of the Wiki and Download Section in early 2007 started a new era for the forum.

OS-tan Collections dominated the early 2000s and onwards in the west that if you use sites to see backlinks and put OS-tan Collection’s URL in, you may just see how big of a dominance it had and still has….

A fandom wiki for tropes has an image of MS-DOS-tan that links back to OSC (source), Vice’s articles about how Internet Explorer isn't the only one with an anime-girl mascot links back to the page for Yu & Ai Madobe (source), the Wikipedia articles for the Worldwide Military Command and Control System links back to WWMCCS-tan’s entry (no joke, look at ref 8 on the page for the WWMCCS source), the Linux Mint forums talk about how Linux Mint doesn’t have a mascot and link back to Touko’s page at OSC (source), the Inkscape wiki used to use C-Chan’s gallery at OSC to show anime-style artwork produced almost entirely in Inkscape (source), the list goes on and on that even the site for MSX-DOS-tan links back to which is in Japanese (source). You can use a site like seomator.com to see more but OSC is essentially “everywhere” in a way.

OS-tan Collections was also responsible for making more OS-tans such as Amiga-tan, more Mac-tans as Futaba only had one for Mac OS X and Mac OS 9 and so on. Go forward to September 16, 2006, in the “OS-tan Fan-Fics, Comics and Fan-Stuff” on the forums there was a post started by C-Chan called “OS-tan Annex Project”. Below are the contents of the main quote of the post:

Hello and welcome everyone to the OS-tan Annex thread. ^__^

Here I've chosen to consolidate all the OS-tan renditions, fanart and contributions that make up the building blocks of the annex to the OS-tan universe that I'm developing. This "Annex" is meant to be a backstory intended to integrate my new OS-tans into the preexisting OS-tan universe without overwriting too much work done by countless other artists. For this reason I've categorized the OS-tans into "factions", not to force political separations on the poor girls, but rather to better organize the storytelling elements. It would certainly be much easier to remember an obscure OS-tan if she has a familiar group representing her. ^__^

The first 17 posts on this thread are where I will be posting my content or any valuable content created by others. So whenever I churn out new drawings, or artists create new OS-tans, or biographies need updating or new OS-tans are suggested, look to these pages for the updated material. I'll likely flag it somehow for easy identification.

All other posts after that are free-for-all, of course. My goal is to get away from posting a thread for each new OS-tan, in order to free up space, ensure that their background info is easily accessible, and (hopefully) streamline the conversation pertaining to new OS-tans or the Annex in general. If this format works, the subsequent posts should make for some very interesting reading. ^__^

Keep in mind that, due to time constraints, these posts are still under construction as of this writing. New OS-tans will only be displayed with their chibi preconcepts (and not with Wiki-style logos as I had hoped I'd have done by now), and it's likely that not every preexisting OS-tan will have her link(s) set up yet. In short, as is always the case with me, this will all look like one giant mess. ^^'

But please bear with me for a while, and I promise this thread will flourish with sweet (56K-friendly) eye-candy one day. ^v^

Sincerely,

C-chan the Pig

OS-tan Collection’s Annex Project is what conveyed OS-tan Collections as a whole. In short, The Annex Project is by no means official but is an expanded universe that strives to integrate the new OS-tan characters and backstories with those of other OS-tan artists while still staying faithful to the original OS-tan "canon" and the work of other artists. Of course, this came with its own faults when the wiki was established in 2007.
