Character

Homeko is the personification of Windows XP Home Edition and the twin sister of XP Pro-tan. Her character evolved from an early suggested design for XP-tan, which was later adapted for the Home Edition. Homeko is characterized as a fujoshi (yaoi fangirl) who draws her own doujinshi and has a strong interest in yaoi/BL media. She is often impulsive and lecherous, with an intense imagination that sometimes annoys others. Homeko rarely goes outside except for doujinshi sales and is sometimes depicted as slovenly with a large appetite similar to her sister, Saseko.

Appearance

Homeko has green hair worn in a short ponytail with large XP-shaped hairclips that cover her ears. She is often depicted in a green-and-white tracksuit, referencing the Home Edition packaging. Occasionally, she wears green cybernetic clothes, especially at conventions and doujinshi events. Her original design was a"android-like" character that did not have a distinctive personality.

According to the Trouble Windows OS-tan Fan Book, Homeko's design is based on an earlier XP design that was made in 2003 when the first designs for Me-tan and 2K-tan were created. Around 10:00 a.m., in response to the ongoing trend, a new "XP personification" appeared. This character was depicted with an image of "cool and high performance," the opposite of Me. The first XP personification, named "Home Edition," was initially treated as a "stepchild" of Windows 2K due to its complicated design, making it difficult to draw.

On January 29, 2005, shortly after the design was named "HOME=Homeko," a thread appeared stating, "I can't help but think of Homeko as a fujoshi who likes 801 doujin." Despite the unexpected direction of the thread, the impact of this name led to a large-scale festival similar to the one held for Inu-T.

While it might seem unimaginable, the peculiar nature of this phenomenon created a unique charm, and the popularity of Homeko soared.

Associations

  • Her twin sister is Saseko while her brother is Homeo, with whom she shares a complex and often humorous relationship.
