Character

Vistake personifies Windows Vista, known for various interpretations in the OS-tan community, including controversial ones. Vistake is often depicted as an abrasive and self-deprecating character with a penchant for painting. She harbors jealousy towards highly skilled artists. Over time, she has become less controversial as her characterization has evolved independently from her origins.

Appearance

Vistake is commonly depicted wearing a red and white sailor fuku with striped stockings. Her grey hair is usually styled in a ponytail held by a screw-resembling hairclip, matching a similar object on her forehead.

Associations

  • Often seen alongside Visbou

    • Notes and Trivia

  • Her name would be written as "Vistake" in English while in Japanese it is written as "Bisutake". The "V" sound is often approximated with a "B" sound in Japanese, which is why you see ビ (bi) used in their names, so most other English based wikis translate only the "bi" part to "vi". Originally, Japanese had no ヴ character so they used variations of ビ (bi). You can still pronounce it as "Vi", but other Japanese wikis tend to use "Bi" instead.
